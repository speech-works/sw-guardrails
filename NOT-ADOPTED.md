# What we did not adopt

Building `sw-guardrails` meant reading widely: the Stuttering Foundation, the
NSA, TISA, STAMMA, ASHA, and the stammering-pride scholarship (all listed in the
[README references](./README.md#references)). Not everything in those sources
made it into the rules. This page records the ideas we found but deliberately
left out, and why, so the choices are transparent and open to challenge.

These are judgement calls, not criticisms of the organisations. Each source is
strong in its own context; the only question here is what fits Speechworks'
product and its India audience. If you think we got one of these wrong, please
[raise an issue](./CONTRIBUTING.md). There is a copy-paste format that makes it
quick.

| What we found | Source | Why we didn't adopt it, and what we do instead |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The position that people who stammer should *not* seek disability or "handicap" status | TISA: [Handicapped?](https://stammer.in/handicapped/), [TISA on Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Indian_Stammering_Association) | A political stance, contested within TISA's own community, that can read as minimising real interview and workplace discrimination. Speechworks stays neutral and agency-centred: we neither lobby for a legal status nor deny that discrimination is real. |
| Framing acceptance through spiritual or religious ideas (self-realisation through self-effort, meaning-making) | TISA: [About](https://stammer.in/about-us-2/) | Resonant for some, but Speechworks serves a religiously and culturally diverse India audience, and prescribing a spiritual frame would exclude people. We keep acceptance secular and plain. |
| "Stammering pride" as the destination everyone should reach | [Did I Stutter?](https://didistutter.org/), [Stammering Pride (STAMMA)](https://stamma.org/features/what-stammering-pride) | We treat affirmation as one valid path, not a requirement (rule A9, self-determination). The critique literature notes the pride frame is often "Western, primarily white and privileged," and pushing it can invalidate people who want fluency tools or simply want to communicate. We lead with dignity and acceptance, which travel better, and never make pride mandatory. |
| The strong social model: stammering is "only" social discrimination, not an impairment | [Did I Stutter?](https://didistutter.org/), [Stuttering and the social model (PubMed)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35248920/) | We hold the biopsychosocial view. Stammering has a real neurological and genetic basis (rule A7) *and* a social and emotional overlay. We don't deny the neurology to make a social point, nor reduce stammering to a defect. |
| War and battle language: "conquer," "attack the iceberg," "adversary," "defeat" | Stuttering Foundation, [Advice to Those Who Stutter](https://www.stutteringhelp.org/sites/default/files/Migrate/book0009_may2010.pdf) | The book's substance is foundational to this skill, but its combative verbs frame stammering as an enemy, which feeds shame. We keep the iceberg image and ban the war metaphors (rule A8). |
| Fluency as the goal, and as the measure of progress | the older "speak-more-fluently" tradition in the same book | This is the core "fluency god" guardrail (rule A2). Fluency may be a side effect, never the headline metric, and frequency of stammering is not progress (NSA, ASHA). |
| The lean that therapy is near-useless because it "almost always ends in relapse" | TISA: [About](https://stammer.in/about-us-2/) | We don't adopt the anti-therapy implication. Therapy and acceptance are compatible (STAMMA, ASHA), and Speechworks is complementary to an SLP and to community, not a substitute and not a reason to skip therapy (rule A6). |
| A US-centric DEI committee and intersectionality apparatus | NSA: [Diversity, Equity & Inclusion](https://www.westutter.org/dei) | We kept the kernel (don't treat people who stutter as a monolith; respect individual difference, rule B4) but did not import a US organisation's internal committee and policy scaffolding wholesale. Speechworks is small and India-focused, so that would be inauthentic. |

## A note on statistics

Some sources cite precise figures (for example, exact relapse percentages or
gene counts) that reached us through secondary or paraphrased material. We did
not embed numbers we could not confirm at the primary source. Where the skill
needs to state cause, it says "neurological and genetic basis" rather than a
specific statistic. If you add a number to the rules, verify it at the original
publication first.
